Fourier Audio Drawing

Draw a single-stroke curve, then play the image as an 8-second looping stereo signal. Left channel follows x, right channel follows y.

Loop 8.0s
Level Auto

Input

Press and drag to draw. Keep it one continuous stroke for the cleanest result.

Fourier View

Epicycles are ordered by amplitude. The cyan trace is the reconstructed curve.

Fourier Series

A closed drawing can be treated as a complex signal z(t) = x(t) + iy(t). Fourier series break that signal into rotating circles at different frequencies. Add enough of them together and the original curve comes back.

Physics

The audio is the same trajectory interpreted as motion through time. The left speaker carries horizontal displacement and the right speaker carries vertical displacement. In physical terms, it is a periodic oscillator with many harmonics superposed into one loop.
